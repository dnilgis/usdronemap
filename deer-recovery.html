Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about drone deer recovery

Drone deer recovery uses thermal imaging cameras mounted on drones to detect the heat signature of a downed deer. The drone flies a systematic search pattern over the area where the deer was last seen, and the thermal camera can detect the animal's body heat even through moderate vegetation. Most recoveries happen within 15-30 minutes of the drone being deployed.
Drone deer recovery typically costs between $75-$300 depending on your location, time of day, and search complexity. Most pilots charge a flat fee for the first hour of search time, with additional fees if extended searching is required. Many offer a "no find, reduced fee" policy.
The best time to call is as soon as possible after losing the blood trail, ideally within 2-6 hours. Thermal imaging works best in the early morning or evening when there's a greater temperature difference between the deer and surroundings. However, drones can locate deer even 12-24 hours later if the body hasn't been scavenged.
Professional drone deer recovery services report success rates between 70-90% when called within 12 hours of the shot. Success rates decrease after 24 hours due to scavenging and body temperature equalization. Factors affecting success include terrain, vegetation density, and weather conditions.
Yes, thermal drones can detect deer through moderate brush and grass. However, dense evergreen canopy or very thick vegetation may block thermal signatures. Experienced pilots know how to adjust flight patterns and altitudes to maximize detection through various cover types.
Yes, most pilots require you to be present to show them the last known location, blood trail, and shot placement. You'll also need to be there to identify and recover the deer once located. The pilot will guide you to the exact GPS coordinates once found.
