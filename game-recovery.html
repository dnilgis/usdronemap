Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about drone game recovery

Thermal drones can locate virtually any downed game animal including whitetail deer, mule deer, elk, moose, wild hogs, black bear, antelope, caribou, and other big game. The thermal camera detects body heat regardless of the animal species, making it effective for all types of game recovery.
Using drones to recover already-downed game is legal in most states, as it's considered a recovery aid rather than hunting assistance. However, some states have restrictions on using drones during active hunting or in certain wildlife areas. Check your state's wildlife regulations or ask your local drone pilot about specific rules.
Drones are extremely effective for wild hog recovery. Hogs often run into thick brush and dense vegetation after being shot. Thermal imaging cuts through the cover and detects the hog's body heat, making recovery much faster than traditional tracking. Many Texas and Southern state hunters now rely on drone recovery services.
Yes, drones are valuable for elk recovery in mountain terrain. The aerial view helps cover steep, difficult-to-access areas quickly. Thermal cameras can spot elk through sparse timber and across canyons. Experienced mountain pilots know how to navigate challenging terrain and wind conditions for successful searches.
Be ready to share: exact GPS coordinates or description of last known location, time of shot, shot placement (vitals, gut, etc.), animal's behavior after the shot (ran, walked, direction traveled), blood trail details, and terrain description. This helps the pilot plan an effective search pattern.
