Why Texas Hunters Need Drone Recovery

Texas presents unique challenges for game recovery. The state's 700,000+ deer hunters and massive wild hog population create constant demand for recovery services. From the impenetrable brush of South Texas to the cedar breaks of Hill Country, traditional tracking methods often fail.

Thermal drone technology has become essential for Texas hunters. The state's long deer season (October through January) and year-round hog hunting mean pilots stay busy helping hunters recover their game.

Wild Hog Recovery in Texas

With an estimated 2.6 million wild hogs causing $500 million in annual agricultural damage, Texas hunters actively pursue these destructive animals. Hogs are notoriously tough and often run deep into thick brush after being hit. Thermal drones have become the go-to solution for hog recovery, especially on large South Texas ranches where wounded hogs can disappear into thousands of acres of brush.

South Texas Trophy Deer

South Texas produces some of America's largest whitetails, with bucks commonly exceeding 150 inches. When a trophy deer is hit, hunters need absolute certainty in recovery. The dense mesquite and prickly pear make ground tracking extremely difficult‚Äîand risky given the rattlesnake population. Thermal drones provide safe, effective recovery without risk to hunters or dogs.

Texas Regulations

Texas allows the use of drones for game recovery after the shot. The state's extended hunting seasons and liberal bag limits mean consistent demand for recovery services throughout fall and winter. Many Texas drone pilots also assist with predator control and ranch management during the off-season.
