Why Michigan Hunters Use Drone Deer Recovery

Michigan's 650,000+ deer hunters make it one of America's top whitetail states. The state's unique geography‚Äîfrom the remote Upper Peninsula wilderness to the agricultural south‚Äîcreates diverse tracking challenges that thermal drones solve.

The UP's vast public lands and thick cedar swamps are notorious for losing deer. Traditional tracking can take hours or fail entirely when deer bed down in impenetrable cover. Southern Michigan's standing corn and bean fields present similar challenges during the early archery and firearm seasons.

Michigan's Extended Deer Season

Michigan offers one of the country's longest deer seasons, running from October through January with archery, firearm, and muzzleloader periods. This extended season means recovery pilots stay busy for months, helping hunters across all weapon types locate their harvests.

Upper Peninsula Challenges

The UP presents unique recovery challenges. Vast tracts of state and national forest, combined with thick tag alder swamps and remote access, make traditional tracking extremely difficult. Many UP deer camps now keep drone pilot contacts on hand, knowing that thermal recovery is often the only reliable option for deer that run into the swamp.

Michigan Regulations

Michigan permits using drones for game recovery after a legal shot. The state's mix of public and private land means pilots must navigate access permissions, but most landowners welcome recovery efforts to prevent waste of game.
